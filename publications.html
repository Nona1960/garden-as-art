Theory of Painting and Contemporary Art

For more than twenty-five years, Professor Nona Dronova has taught professional disciplines at the Higher School of Folk Arts (St. Petersburg), combining academic teaching with research in contemporary painting, decorative art and artistic philosophy.

Her published works develop an original theoretical framework for contemporary painting, examining colour as an independent artistic language, the philosophy of decorative form and the relationship between craftsmanship, artistic thinking and visual perception. Particular attention is given to the role of brushwork, layered painting techniques, chromatic structure, rhythm, abstraction and the transformation of natural forms into painterly language.

The publications also address fundamental issues facing contemporary art: the boundaries between professional and amateur painting, the influence of digital technologies on artistic quality, the philosophy of post-symbolism, and the preservation of artistic standards in an era of rapidly changing visual culture.

Based on many years of artistic practice and academic research, these studies propose a coherent theoretical model in which painting is understood not as the imitation of reality, but as an independent philosophical system governed by the laws of colour, light, rhythm and visual harmony.
